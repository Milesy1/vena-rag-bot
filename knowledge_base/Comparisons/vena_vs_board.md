## What Is Board?
Board is an all-in-one enterprise planning platform that combines BI, corporate performance management (CPM), and predictive analytics. It’s designed to give organizations a centralized space to plan, analyze and report across business units.
Unlike tools that specialize in a single function, Board is a modular platform—you choose the components you need and stitch them together into a custom solution. That means deep configurability, but also longer implementation timelines, more technical upkeep, and a steeper learning curve.
Board is often positioned for midsize and enterprise organizations with dedicated IT or analytics resources to maintain and evolve the platform over time.

### Where Board Falls Short
Board offers a flexible and powerful platform for business intelligence, analytics and planning—but that depth comes with some tradeoffs, especially for teams looking for speed, transparency, or ease of use.
Based on verified user reviews, several recurring challenges stand out:
#### 1. Licensing Limits and Feature Restrictions
One of the most common concerns among users is that the platform’s licensing model feels restrictive. While Board does offer tiered access, the lowest-level licenses provide limited functionality, which can hamper broader team adoption or require upgrading just to access basic features.
#### 2. Lack of Transparency in Data Processing
Users also report that while data ingestion and calculations are technically easy to set up, the platform often lacks visibility into what’s happening under the hood. This makes troubleshooting or debugging workflows difficult, especially during development or when working with large datasets.
“Calculations are easy to implement, but it lacks visibility on the intermediary steps/results, which can be useful when developing new things.” — G2 Reviewer
Board’s cloud logs don’t always show clear processes—so instead of clear diagnostics, users are left guessing where things went wrong.
“It is hard to see what exactly has happened. You can only make a more educated guess.” — G2 Reviewer
#### 3. Board Users Continue To Use Excel
Ironically, despite being a platform designed to go beyond spreadsheets, some users find themselves returning to Excel for formatting, visual polish and certain data handling capabilities. Board lets you prepare presentations, but lacks the intuitive customization and flexibility teams expect.
“We use Board to create Excel for tables and schemes for final PowerPoint presentations... but Board does not have as many aspect solutions as Excel.” — G2 Reviewer
While Board may work well for organizations with dedicated technical teams, these concerns highlight areas where it may slow down teams that need agility, transparency or broader usability across departments.

### Customization
Vena’s offers stand-out flexibility, allowing teams to tailor models, templates, and workflows to their unique business needs as they evolve—without heavy reliance on IT. Whether you’re planning for workforce expenses, revenue drivers, or multi-entity consolidations, Vena adapts to your structure.

Getting data into the system is often the first hurdle in any software implementation. Vena simplifies this with seamless import tools and prebuilt connectors. Board’s integration options, while powerful, require more effort to configure, according to users.
