# Vena vs. Prophix: Comparing Features, Integrations, Reviews and More
In some respects, it’s never been easier to be a member of a finance team or a CFO. That’s because there are so many tools on the market that can help make the work more fluid and efficient than ever before.
However, the process of choosing the right platform for your corporate performance management (CPM) needs can present its own challenges. It can take a long time, for starters, since there are so many potential solutions on the market—each with its own strengths and weaknesses.
You’ll want to do your due diligence, interact with as many tools as you can and figure out which features are critical to your unique business’s success. You’re not trying to create more work for yourself, after all—but making a wise choice of CPM provider may require a little heavy lifting.
We want to shoulder a bit of that burden. That’s why in this blog, we’re comparing two of the most popular FP&A solutions choices on the market: Vena vs. Prophix.
Read on to see how Vena and Prophix stack up against one another on several fronts, including key features, integrations, ease of use and more.

## How the Technology Works
Broadly, Vena offers organizations a single unified platform for many use cases, all built around a native Excel interface. Vena’s online analytical processing (OLAP) database technology enables multidimensional analysis of large volumes of business data. In a nutshell, that means users can analyze their data through several different lenses.
Vena is built to easily combine various data sources, bringing them together for categorization and analysis. This is particularly useful in CPM because it lets you find the data you need from across your data sources to model various scenarios, create budgets, make forecasts, etc. with not only financial data, but operational data too. It also requires less manual maintenance, with no admin necessary to create an intersection of data before it becomes viewable.
And, as mentioned, Vena also natively integrates with Excel, which means that your established workflow doesn’t need to change much, if at all.

### Prophix
The broad concept behind Prophix, from a design standpoint, was to concentrate regular business application tasks into a central application and empower non-technical users. It’s good to note, too, that Prophix recently upgraded to a new and improved product, Prophix One, that built on the existing platform and added new capabilities and tools, such as account reconciliation and disclosure management.
On a more granular level, Prophix uses OLAP technology to allow users to manipulate data. This works with the platform’s “cubes,” which house calculation results for planning models. In contrast to Vena, Prophix users create a standalone cube for each use case.
Prophix does not have a native Excel interface, though it has an add-in that can connect the FP&A platform to users’ existing workbooks for the purposes of collecting inputs from budget contributors outside of Finance, but not for carrying out your work in Excel.

## Overall Review: Which Is Best for Your Team?
It’s fair to say that Vena and Prophix both offer prospective customers a full-service FP&A platform that can tick many, if not most organization’s boxes. However, there are some key differences in the underlying technology and additional areas that are important to keep in mind.
Perhaps most notably, Vena’s native Excel integration—which ultimately provides greater ease of use and adoption for many finance teams—could be important to many companies. Prophix, in contrast, has an Excel add-in, which is designed for collecting inputs from budget contributors, not necessarily for finance teams to use for modeling purposes. As a result, it may feel restrictive to some users.
Additionally, there are some other things to note when deciding between the two. Vena is cloud-based first and foremost, for one, whereas Prophix has limited cloud data integration technology—customers may have a hard time migrating from an on-site solution to the cloud.
AI capabilities are another area to think about. While Prophix did roll out an AI assistant years ago, its current AI capabilities are somewhat lackluster compared to Vena’s two AI tools, and Vena Copilot, in particular.
Prophix’s underlying architecture also requires users to create cubes for each use case, which can muddle processes, whereas users may find Vena’s approach through CubeFLEX, our unified analytical database technology, may be more streamlined and efficient.
With that all in mind, Vena is a strong fit for companies of all sizes looking for a comprehensive and flexible FP&A platform—particularly those operating on Microsoft 365. Vena can support use cases across not just FP&A-led processes, but financial close management and operational planning too, which means the platform can grow alongside your business as you scale.
Prophix has many advantages and upsides, but it lacks the flexibility and performance needed to handle use cases of higher complexity. As a result, Prophix may be a better fit for mid-size and smaller companies.
No matter which platform your company or organization decides to use, if you’re starting from just having disconnected Excel sheets, you’re likely to give your FP&A team a boost. Nonetheless, it’s important to take note of where your chosen platform excels (pardon the pun) or comes up short to ensure you’re choosing a solution that’s going to work for you over the long haul.
